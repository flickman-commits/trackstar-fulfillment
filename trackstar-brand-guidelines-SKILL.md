# Trackstar Brand Guidelines

Reference document for all Trackstar-branded output. Consult before creating any visual, written, or design work.

---

## 1. Brand Identity

### What Trackstar Is
Personalized race finisher prints — premium wall art customized with a runner's finish time, pace, bib number, course map, and race-day weather. Printed on archival matte fine art paper, professionally framed, shipped to the customer's doorstep.

### What Trackstar Is NOT
- Not a participation certificate or generic race poster
- Not a finisher medal alternative
- Not a mass-produced souvenir
- Not clip art, not cheap, not casual

### Brand Positioning
**"Celebrating athletic achievement."** Trackstar sits at the intersection of fine art and personal achievement. The product is a trophy you hang on your wall — something worthy of the effort that earned it.

### Target Audience — Two Segments
1. **Runners (self-purchase):** Serious recreational runners who view running as identity. They chase PRs, remember split times, and want their achievement displayed with the same quality they put into the training. They convert when proud of their time.
2. **Supporters (gift-purchase):** Partners, parents, friends buying for a runner. They convert in the emotional window post-finish — the "I'm so proud of them" moment. They need the product to feel like the most thoughtful gift imaginable without requiring running knowledge to buy.

## 2. Color Palette

### Primary Colors

| Name | Hex | RGB | Usage |
|------|-----|-----|-------|
| **Trackstar Dark** | `#1A1A1A` | 26, 26, 26 | Primary background for posters, emails, dark UI, QR codes |
| **Cream / Off-White** | `#F7F5F0` | 247, 245, 240 | Poster mat border, light backgrounds, body text on dark |
| **Pure White** | `#FFFFFF` | 255, 255, 255 | Website backgrounds, clean layouts |

### Secondary Colors

| Name | Hex | RGB | Usage |
|------|-----|-----|-------|
| **Warm Terracotta** | `#C8553D` | 200, 85, 61 | Accent color — CTAs, headings in docs, email buttons, highlights. Use sparingly. |
| **Medium Gray** | `#666666` | 102, 102, 102 | Secondary text, captions, metadata |
| **Light Gray** | `#999999` | 153, 153, 153 | Tertiary text, subtle borders |
| **Border Gray** | `#DDDDDD` | 221, 221, 221 | Table borders, dividers, hairlines |

### Race-Specific Poster Colors
Each poster design has a **dominant dark background color** that reflects the race's geographic identity — not necessarily `#1A1A1A`. Examples:
- Marine Corps Marathon: deep navy/midnight (D.C. monuments at dusk)
- CIM: Sacramento green-gold tones
- Oakland: Bay-area blue-dark
- Kiawah Island: ocean-dark teal

The dark background varies per race but always stays in the **dark** range (never light, never pastel). The route line "glows" warm against this darkness.

### Color Rules
- **Dark mode is default.** Trackstar's visual identity lives in dark backgrounds with light typography. This is the signature look.
- **Cream, not white, for the mat border.** The off-white `#F7F5F0` mat inside the frame is a signature element — it reads "gallery quality," not "office printer."
- **Terracotta for CTAs and accents only.** Don't flood layouts with it. A single button, a heading underline, a highlight — that's the right dose.
- **No neon, no bright primaries, no gradients on the brand itself.** The only color "pop" comes from the glowing route line on the poster, which uses warm tones (gold, amber, orange) per race.

## 3. Typography

### Poster Typography
- **Title font (race name):** Bold condensed sans-serif, all caps. Should feel authoritative and architectural — like the name of the race belongs carved in stone. <!-- PLACEHOLDER: Confirm exact typeface with Dan — likely Bebas Neue, Oswald, or a custom condensed face -->
- **Stats trio (pace / weather / date):** Light-weight monospace or tabular font, positioned below the title. Small, precise, data-forward. <!-- PLACEHOLDER: Confirm exact typeface -->
- **Personalization (name + bib):** Clean sans-serif, anchored bottom-right of the composition.
- **Hierarchy:** Race name dominates → course map is the hero visual → stats trio provides data texture → personalization anchors bottom-right.

### Web & Marketing Typography
- **Headings:** Clean sans-serif (the Shopify site currently uses the theme's default system font stack). Keep it simple — no decorative or script fonts.
- **Body copy:** System sans-serif. Readable, not decorative.
- **Email / Klaviyo:** Arial or system sans-serif. Dark text on light backgrounds, or cream/white text on `#1A1A1A` backgrounds.

### Typography Rules
- **ALL CAPS for race names on posters and in headline treatments.** Lowercase for body copy and descriptions.
- **No script fonts, no handwriting fonts, no novelty fonts.** Ever.
- **Monospace for data/stats** where it reinforces the "precision" feel (pace, time, weather).
- **Generous letter-spacing on headlines** — the poster designs use tracked-out caps, not tight kerning.

## 4. Poster Design System — "The Trackstar Five"

Every poster follows these five signature design elements:

### 4.1 The Cream Mat
Off-white border inside the frame, emulating a gallery-quality mat. This is the first visual signal that says "this is art, not a printout."

### 4.2 The Stats Trio
Pace / Weather / Date displayed in light monospace below the race title. These three data points ground the poster in the specific day the runner achieved something. They're small but essential — removing them would make it feel generic.

### 4.3 The Glowing Route
The race course map rendered as a warm-toned route line (gold/amber/orange family) against the dark background. This is the visual hero of the poster — it occupies roughly 50–60% of the composition. The "glow" effect gives it presence without being garish.

### 4.4 Shadow Landmarks
Subtle cityscape silhouettes, iconic landmarks, or geographic features at 10–15% opacity in the background. These anchor the poster in place — you shouldn't consciously notice them at first glance, but they make the poster feel "right" for that city. Examples: D.C. monuments, Sacramento capitol dome, San Francisco skyline.

### 4.5 Bottom-Right Personalization
Runner's name and bib number anchored in the lower-right quadrant. This is the "this is mine" element. It's positioned to be discovered second — after the course map catches the eye — creating a moment of personal recognition.

### Poster Specs
- **Orientation:** Portrait, always
- **Aspect ratio:** 2:3
- **Sizes:** 8×10, 12×18, 16×24, 24×36
- **Print medium:** Archival matte fine art paper
- **Frame options:** Black or oak wood frame with real glass
- **Background:** One dominant dark color per race reflecting geographic identity

## 5. Photography & Product Imagery

### Product Photography Style
- **Hero shots:** Framed poster at an angle, lifestyle context (leaning against a wall, on a shelf, in a living room). Warm ambient lighting. The room should feel like a real home, not a studio.
- **Flat lay / straight-on:** Clean, centered, minimal styling. Dark background or neutral surface.
- **Unboxing moments:** Hands holding the poster, genuine reaction. These are UGC-style but should still feel elevated.

### What Works in Ads (Proven)
- **Product photo, no text overlay, dark background.** This is the #1 performing ad format — the poster itself is the creative. Don't clutter it with text, badges, or overlays.
- **UGC testimonial videos** — authentic, not overproduced. The "Mom UGC" format (real person, real story, real reaction) dramatically outperforms polished brand videos.
- **Before/after or comparison framing** — medal in a drawer vs. poster on a wall.

### What Doesn't Work
- Text-heavy ad images
- Bright/white backgrounds (kills the premium feel)
- Generic stock lifestyle imagery
- Over-produced brand videos that feel like commercials
- "Congratulations!" energy in the visuals

## 6. Brand Voice & Copy

### Tone
**Restrained. Data-forward. Proud.** Trackstar's voice is the voice of someone who respects the achievement but doesn't shout about it. Think: the coach who gives you a nod at the finish line, not the announcer screaming your name.

### Voice Principles

| Principle | Do | Don't |
|-----------|-----|-------|
| **Earned, not given** | "You earned the wall space." | "Congratulations on your amazing achievement!" |
| **Trophy, not certificate** | "This is a trophy, not a certificate." | "Commemorate your special day!" |
| **Data as emotion** | "2:58:42. October 27th. 52°F and overcast." | "Remember that magical fall morning!" |
| **Runner as identity** | "For the Dad who doesn't quit." | "Great gift for active people!" |
| **Show, don't tell** | Let the product image speak. Minimal copy. | Wall of text explaining why it's premium. |
| **Gift-buyer empathy** | "Make you look like the most thoughtful gift-giver alive." | "The perfect present for any occasion!" |

### Copy Formulas That Work

**Headlines (short, punchy):**
- "Your miles. Your wall."
- "Medals go in drawers. This goes on your wall."
- "Finish lines look better framed."
- "He ran it. Let him hang it." (Father's Day)
- "For the Dad who doesn't quit." (Father's Day)
- "You earned the wall space."

**Product descriptions (PDP pattern):**
> Trackstar's [Race Name] print is a customizable print featuring your runner's name, official time, pace, and race-day weather. This thoughtful and beautifully made race print is the best and easiest way to show your runner that you love them.
>
> Each poster is personalized with: the runner's name, bib number, finish time, pace, the official race date, actual race-day weather, and a beautifully illustrated race course map.
>
> Choose from framed or unframed, and we'll handle the rest. Just give us the runner's name and we'll make you look like the most thoughtful gift-giver alive.

**Licensed vs. unlicensed language:**
- Licensed: "Trackstar is the Official Race Print Partner of the [Race Name]."
- Unlicensed: "*Disclaimer: This artwork is an independent commemorative piece and is not affiliated with or endorsed by the [Race Name]."

**Ad copy pattern (geo-targeted):**
- Pre-race: "Running the [Race Name]?"
- Post-race: "Just finished the [Race Name]? You deserve this."

**Email / SMS tone:**
- Short sentences. Punchy. No fluff.
- Lead with the emotional hook, not the product feature.
- One CTA per message. Don't dilute attention.

### Words & Phrases to Use
- "Personalized race print" (not poster, when possible — "print" sounds more premium)
- "Finisher print" (reinforces achievement)
- "Your race. Your wall."
- "Celebrating athletic achievement"
- "Made to order"
- "Archival matte fine art paper"
- "Pro-grade frames"
- "Official Race Print Partner" (licensed only)
- "100% Accuracy Guarantee"

### Words & Phrases to Avoid
- "Congrats!" / "Congratulations!" (too generic, participation-trophy energy)
- "Souvenir" / "keepsake" / "memento" (too casual — this is a trophy)
- "Poster" in isolation (use "print" or "race print" — "poster" cheapens it)
- "Limited edition" (it's not — every print is custom)
- "Perfect gift for runners!" (too broad, no identity)
- Exclamation marks in headlines (one max per page — restraint is brand)
- "Amazing" / "awesome" / "incredible" (filler words with no specificity)

## 7. Logo Usage

### The Wordmark
Trackstar uses a clean sans-serif wordmark (the word "Trackstar" in a specific typeface). Two versions:
- **Dark version:** `#1A1A1A` text on light backgrounds
- **Light version:** White/cream text on dark backgrounds

### Logo Rules
- Minimum clear space: height of the "T" on all sides
- Never stretch, rotate, or distort
- Never place on busy backgrounds without sufficient contrast
- Never recreate in a different font — use the actual logo file
- The logo files live in Google Drive (Trackstar folder)

## 8. Channel-Specific Guidelines

### Shopify Website (trackstar.art)
- Dark product images on the PDP (poster on dark or lifestyle background)
- Announcement bar: "Free US Shipping on orders over $100" + "Buy 2 prints & get the second 30% off"
- Hero: "Personalized Race Prints" with "Celebrating X runners and counting" as social proof
- Licensed partner logo bar below the fold (scrolling ticker)
- Hall of Fame leaderboard (fastest times through orders) — gamification element
- Customer photo gallery with city captions (UGC proof)
- FAQ uses accordion/collapsible format

### Klaviyo (Email & SMS)
- Email background: `#1A1A1A` or white — no mid-grays
- Button color: Terracotta `#C8553D` on dark backgrounds, or `#1A1A1A` on light
- Font: Arial (system default for email reliability)
- Pop-up form: Dark background, cream text, minimal — "You earned it. Now hang it." or similar short headline
- SMS: Plain text, no emojis (or max 1), direct link. "Your [Race] finisher print is ready. 15% off expires tonight: [link]"

### Meta Ads
- **Creative:** Product photo on dark background, no text overlay = highest performer
- **Ad copy:** Short, race-specific. Pre-race vs. post-race variants.
- **UGC:** Real people, real reactions. Mom/Dad/partner gifting angle is proven.
- **Landing page:** Always the race-specific Shopify PDP, never the homepage (unless running a sitewide sale)
- **Advantage+ enhancements:** Turn OFF all except Translate — visual touch-ups distort the poster creative

### Instagram (@trackstar_art)
- Feed: Product shots, customer UGC reposts, new design reveals
- Stories: Race-day content, unboxing reactions, behind-the-scenes
- Reels: UGC testimonials, comparison hooks ("medal in a drawer" vs. "print on a wall")
- Giveaway collabs: Partner with race orgs for IG giveaways as activation touchpoint
- Tone: Casual but not sloppy. Short captions. Let images lead.

### Race Partner Materials (B2B)
- **Proposals:** Professional but not corporate. Use the Notion template with embedded poster images and unboxing videos. Lead with the payout scenario math — that's what race directors look at first.
- **Partner Toolkit:** Copy-paste-ready promotional templates. Every link UTM-tagged. Simple yes/no decisions for the race director.
- **Flyers / Expo materials:** Product image dominant, QR code prominent, minimal text. CTA: "Scan to get your personalized finisher print" or similar.
- **Samples:** Framed prints shipped to race orgs as proof of quality. These sell the deal — always send samples when possible.

## 9. Print & Physical Materials

### QR Codes
- Scale: 10 (300 DPI equivalent)
- Dark color: `#1A1A1A`
- Background: Transparent (for overlay on any surface)
- Border: 2 (minimal quiet zone)

### Expo / Booth
- Framed sample prints on easels as hero visual
- Dark tablecloth / backdrop to match brand aesthetic
- Flyers: one-sided, product image + QR code + short CTA
- Table cards: small standup with QR for SMS capture
- Banner: product image at scale, QR code, "Scan for your finisher print"

### Packaging
- Prints shipped by Artelo (vendor) — printed and framed in Kentucky
- Unboxing experience matters — the reveal moment is content-worthy (UGC driver)

## 11. Quick Reference — "Is This On-Brand?" Checklist

Before shipping any Trackstar-branded output, check:

- [ ] **Dark, not light?** The default visual mode is dark backgrounds with light type.
- [ ] **Restrained, not loud?** One CTA, minimal text, let the product speak.
- [ ] **Specific, not generic?** Reference the actual race, city, or data — not "your marathon."
- [ ] **Proud, not excited?** Nod at the finish line, don't scream. No "CONGRATS!" energy.
- [ ] **Premium, not casual?** "Print" not "poster." "Trophy" not "souvenir." Archival paper, pro frames.
- [ ] **Runner as identity?** Copy speaks to someone who views running as who they are, not just what they do.
- [ ] **Gift-buyer empathy?** If targeting supporters, does it make them feel like a genius gift-giver?
- [ ] **Data grounded?** Use real numbers: finish time, pace, weather, race date. Data is emotional.
- [ ] **Right logo version?** Dark logo on light, light logo on dark. Never distorted.
- [ ] **UTM-tagged?** Every link should be trackable. No naked URLs in partner materials.
